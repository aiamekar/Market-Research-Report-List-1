<p><p>The gastrointestinal bleeding treatment market is divided into upper and lower GI tract segments. Upper GI tract treatments address bleeding originating from the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum, often utilizing endoscopic procedures, medications, and surgical interventions. In contrast, lower GI tract treatments focus on bleeding from the small intestine and colon, employing techniques like colonoscopy, cauterization, and resection. Both segments involve a variety of diagnostic tools and therapeutic methods tailored to specific causes and locations of bleeding within the GI tract.</p></p>
