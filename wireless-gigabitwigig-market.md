<p><p>Wireless Gigabit, or WiGig, refers to high-speed wireless technology that provides data transfer rates up to 7 Gbps, primarily through the 802.11ad standard operating in the 60 GHz frequency band. This enables ultra-fast connections for applications like virtual reality and high-definition streaming. In contrast, 802.11ac operates in the 5 GHz band and offers speeds up to 1.3 Gbps. The market types reflect different use cases, with WiGig catering to high-bandwidth applications and 802.11ac serving broader connectivity needs in various environments.</p></p>
