<p><p>The industrial lanolin market primarily consists of two types: anhydrous lanolin and hydrous lanolin. Anhydrous lanolin is a refined, moisture-free variant, widely used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals for its emollient properties. Hydrous lanolin contains water, making it ideal for applications requiring additional hydration, such as creams and ointments. Both types are derived from sheep wool and are valued for their skin-nourishing qualities, making them essential ingredients in various personal care products and industrial applications.</p></p>
